Career Start

This is a report summarizing the things I did to get a job in high performance computing.

Project description

Halfway through my final semester at EPFL, I realized that my best skill was mathematics, and not programming. The issue is that I wanted to be a programmer, not a mathematician. This project was an attempt to fix that.

The goals were the following.

  1. Learn how to actually write parallel code.
  2. Revisit my \(n\)-queens project and make it faster.

I gave myself eight weeks for part (1). I would complete the eight exercises in Aalto University's CS-E4580 while casually reading up on modern C++.

1. Learning Parallel Programming.

Week 1 - Introduction & Prerequisite Test

I learned in essence that the history of computing was characterised by the fast improvements of transistor density, clock speeds, and instruction latency. Now in the modern era, better performance can be extracted by increasing instruction throughput through various levels of parallelism.
